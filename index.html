The Completed Naawi-Oodena

Source: CNN - Canada's largest urban reserve opens in Winnipeg (2023)

Naawi-Oodena ("Centre of the Heart") is now a vibrant 156-acre urban reserve on Winnipeg's former Kapyong Barracks site. This $1.2 billion development represents the largest single return of urban land to First Nations in Canadian history, developed through a partnership between seven First Nations and various governments.

The completed community houses over 8,000 residents and features commercial, cultural, and community spaces blending Indigenous traditions with modern urban living. The architecture reflects Indigenous values, with buildings oriented to the four directions and public art sharing local Indigenous stories.

Residential Living

Our residential areas now house over 8,000 residents in diverse, inclusive neighborhoods planned using Indigenous design principles. The mix includes single-family homes, townhouses, apartments, and elder-friendly units.

All homes feature energy-efficient designs incorporating traditional knowledge, with community gardens and shared gathering spaces that encourage connection.

View Residential Areas

Thriving Commercial District

Our 1.5 million sq ft commercial space has become an economic engine while maintaining strong Indigenous identity. The district includes Indigenous-owned businesses, a food hall, offices, and cultural spaces.

The commercial area attracts over 1 million visitors annually to experience authentic Indigenous commerce and culture, with incubator programs supporting new Indigenous businesses.

Vibrant Community Spaces

Our cultural and community facilities serve as the heart of Naawi-Oodena, including an Indigenous Cultural Centre, gathering plaza, parks, schools, and healing centers.

These spaces host daily cultural activities and seasonal celebrations, designed with sacred geometry and traditional architectural elements.

Sustainability Achievements

Environmental Milestones

Naawi-Oodena has achieved ambitious sustainability goals, blending Indigenous stewardship with modern ecological design.

Key achievements include LEED Gold certification, 100% renewable energy, 90% waste diversion, and innovative stormwater management combining traditional and modern techniques.
