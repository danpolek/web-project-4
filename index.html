About Naawi-Oodena

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.8

Naawi-Oodena ("Centre of the Heart" in Anishinaabemowin) is a transformative 156-acre urban reserve development on the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg. This $1 billion project represents the largest single return of urban land to First Nations in Canadian history.

The development is led by Treaty One Nation, representing seven Anishinaabe First Nations: Brokenhead Ojibway Nation, Long Plain First Nation, Peguis First Nation, Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation, Sagkeeng First Nation, Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation, and Swan Lake First Nation.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.4-5

Our Vision

Creating a vibrant, sustainable community that honors Indigenous traditions while providing modern urban living through Indigenous-led design principles.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.10

Strategic Location

Centrally located at the former Kapyong Barracks site, bordered by Kenaston Boulevard, Grant Avenue, Taylor Avenue, and the Canadian Pacific Railway.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.7

Economic Impact

The development is projected to generate $2.8 billion in economic impact over 20 years, creating 10,000 construction jobs and 4,000 permanent positions.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.12

Development Areas

Residential Living

Approximately 3,000 housing units across various typologies including single-family homes, townhouses, stacked townhouses, and mid-rise apartments. About 30% of units will be designated as affordable housing.

Key features include Indigenous design principles, energy efficiency, and access to community amenities.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.14-16

Commercial Mixed-Use

1.2 million square feet of retail, office, and institutional space with a focus on Indigenous entrepreneurship. The commercial district will include:

  • Business incubator for Indigenous entrepreneurs
  • Food hall showcasing Indigenous cuisine
  • Cultural retail spaces
  • Professional offices

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.18-20

Community & Cultural Spaces

The cultural heart of Naawi-Oodena will feature:

  • 30,000 sq ft Indigenous Cultural Centre
  • Central Gathering Plaza for ceremonies and events
  • 15 acres of parks with Indigenous landscaping
  • Interpretive trails with Indigenous art installations
  • K-12 school operated by Treaty One Nation

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.22-24

Sustainable Development

Environmental Stewardship

Naawi-Oodena is targeting LEED Gold certification for all major buildings with features including:

  • District geothermal heating system
  • 100% renewable energy by 2030
  • Zero waste to landfill target
  • 40% tree canopy coverage
  • Stormwater management using naturalized wetlands

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.26-28

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.27

Development Timeline

Phase 1 (2022-2025)

  • Infrastructure development
  • First residential units
  • Initial commercial spaces

Phase 2 (2025-2030)

  • Cultural center construction
  • Major residential expansion
  • Community amenities completion

Phase 3 (2030-2040)

  • Final build-out
  • Commercial district completion
  • Full community realization

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.30-32
