About Naawi-Oodena

Naawi-Oodena is a unique urban reserve located on the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg, Manitoba. Led by Treaty One Nations, it symbolizes peace, unity, and reconciliation. "Naawi-Oodena" means "Centre of the Heart" in Anishinaabemowin, and it reflects the vision of Indigenous-led development.

Source: Treaty1.ca

Why Naawi-Oodena Matters

This site sets a new standard for Indigenous urban development. It empowers local communities, honors history, and strengthens the connection between Indigenous culture and the land.

What's unique is that Naawi-Oodena combines First Nations traditional values and cultures with modern city planning processes like zoning, roads, and green infrastructure.

Source: Treaty One on YouTube

The Finished Community

Naawi-Oodena has become a thriving, modern community that blends Indigenous culture with urban living. As Canada's largest urban reserve, it now stands as a powerful symbol of reconciliation and self-determination for the Treaty One First Nations.

Residential Success

The residential area features a wide variety of homes, including detached houses, townhomes, stacked townhouses, and apartment buildings. Families of all sizes found suitable housing, with many units designed to be affordable and inclusive.

Economic Growth

The commercial district has attracted over 150 Indigenous-owned businesses, creating employment opportunities and serving as an economic engine for the region.

Cultural Preservation

The Cultural Campus hosts regular events, language classes, and traditional knowledge sharing, ensuring Indigenous culture remains at the heart of the community.
