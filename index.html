Welcome to Naawi-Oodena

Canada's largest urban reserve for peace and unity

Naawi-Oodena is a transformative 156-acre urban reserve development on the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg, Manitoba. As the largest urban reserve in Canada, this $1 billion project represents a landmark achievement in Indigenous-led development and urban planning. The name "Naawi-Oodena" translates to "Centre of the Heart" in Anishinaabemowin, reflecting both its geographic centrality in Winnipeg and its spiritual significance to Treaty One Nations.

Source: Treaty1.ca - Naawi-Oodena

The development is led by Treaty One Nation, a collective of seven Anishinaabe First Nations: Brokenhead Ojibway Nation, Long Plain First Nation, Peguis First Nation, Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation, Sagkeeng First Nation, Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation, and Swan Lake First Nation. This project represents the largest single return of urban land to First Nations in Canadian history.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan, p.4

Main Development Areas

Residential Living

The residential component will feature approximately 3,000 housing units across various typologies including single-family homes, townhouses, stacked townhouses, and mid-rise apartments. About 30% of units will be designated as affordable housing.

Source: Master Plan, p.12

Mixed-use Commercial Space

The commercial district will include 1.2 million square feet of retail, office, and institutional space, with a focus on Indigenous entrepreneurship. Key features include a business incubator, food hall showcasing Indigenous cuisine, and cultural retail spaces.

Source: Treaty1 Economic Development

Community and Culture

The cultural heart of the development includes:

  • 30,000 sq ft Indigenous Cultural Centre
  • Central Gathering Plaza for ceremonies and events
  • 15 acres of parks with Indigenous landscaping
  • Interpretive trails with Indigenous art installations

Source: Master Plan, p.18-20

Recreation and Sports

The development includes a 100,000 sq ft community recreation complex with aquatic center, gymnasium, and outdoor sports fields. A network of 8km of multi-use trails will connect all areas of the development.

Source: Master Plan, p.22

Education and Learning

The education precinct includes a K-12 school operated by Treaty One Nation, adult education center, and partnerships with local universities for Indigenous-focused programming. The literacy rate goal is 100% for residents.

Source: Treaty1 Education

Why Naawi-Oodena Matters

Naawi-Oodena represents a new model for urban Indigenous economic development in Canada. The project is expected to generate $2.8 billion in economic impact over 20 years, creating 10,000 construction jobs and 4,000 permanent positions. Importantly, 60% of these opportunities will be reserved for Indigenous workers and businesses.

Source: Economic Impact Study 2021

The development incorporates Indigenous design principles throughout, including: - Circular site planning reflecting traditional camp layouts - Buildings oriented to the four directions - Use of natural materials like wood and stone - Integration of Indigenous art and symbolism - Landscaping with medicinal plants and traditional food sources

Source: Master Plan, p.30-32

Environmentally, Naawi-Oodena is targeting LEED Gold certification for all major buildings. The plan includes: - District geothermal heating system - 100% renewable energy by 2030 - Zero waste to landfill target - 40% tree canopy coverage - Stormwater management using naturalized wetlands

Source: Treaty1 Environmental Policy

The Finished Community

Upon completion in 2040, Naawi-Oodena will be home to approximately 10,000 residents with a daytime population of 15,000. The development will feature complete walkability with all amenities within a 15-minute walk from any residence. The street network prioritizes pedestrians and cyclists, with limited vehicle access in the residential core areas.

Source: Master Plan, p.40

The residential areas are organized into distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character: - The Meadows: Affordable housing and seniors living - The Bluffs: Mid-rise apartments and townhomes - The Ridge: Single-family homes with larger lots - The Grove: Mixed-income housing near the cultural center

Source: Master Plan, p.44-46

The commercial district will include: - 500,000 sq ft of retail space - 300,000 sq ft of office space - 200,000 sq ft of light industrial - 200,000 sq ft of institutional space - 100-room boutique hotel with conference facilities

Source: Economic Impact Study, p.15

Transportation infrastructure includes: - Direct access to Winnipeg's rapid transit network - Bike share program with 30 stations - Car share program with electric vehicles - Dedicated lanes for public transit - Underground parking in commercial areas

Source: Master Plan, p.50
