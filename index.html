Welcome to the Naawi-Oodena Page

Naawi-Oodena is an extraordinary urban reserve located on the former site of the Kapyong Barracks in Winnipeg, Manitoba. This visionary project is led by the Treaty One Nations and is a powerful symbol of peace, unity, and reconciliation. The name "Naawi-Oodena" means "Centre of the Heart" in Anishinaabemowin, reflecting its role as a vibrant hub for Indigenous culture and community.

Main Development Areas

  • Residential Living: Spanning over 160 acres, Naawi-Oodena is Canada's largest urban reserve. It will feature modern and sustainable housing for Indigenous and non-Indigenous families alike.
  • Mixed-use Commercial Space: A wide variety of businesses, shops, and restaurants will help support economic growth and provide job opportunities.
  • Community and Culture: Cultural centers, gathering spaces, and traditional teachings will ensure that Indigenous heritage remains at the heart of Naawi-Oodena.
  • Recreation and Sports: Parks, arenas, and green spaces will support healthy lifestyles and connect people through physical activity.
  • Education and Learning: Schools, training centres, and mentorship programs will empower youth and adults through knowledge and opportunity.

Why Naawi-Oodena Matters

This development is not only a place to live and work—it’s a movement toward reconciliation and self-determination. By creating a thriving urban reserve in the heart of Winnipeg, the Treaty One Nations are building a future rooted in respect, sustainability, and culture.

Naawi-Oodena represents a model for Indigenous-led development that uplifts both the local community and the broader region.
