Welcome to Naawi-Oodena

Naawi-Oodena is an extraordinary urban reserve located on the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg, Manitoba. Led by Treaty One Nations, it symbolizes peace, unity, and reconciliation. "Naawi-Oodena" means "Centre of the Heart" in Anishinaabemowin, and it reflects the vision of Indigenous-led development.

Source: Treaty1.ca

Main Development Areas

  • Residential Living: A welcoming neighborhood for Indigenous and non-Indigenous residents alike.
  • Mixed-use Commercial Space: Shops, restaurants, and services that create economic opportunities. The name "Naawi-Oodena" is an Anishinaabe term that translates to "center of the heart and community."
  • Community and Culture: Gathering spaces to celebrate Indigenous identity, stories, and traditions. Three public or cultural spaces found in the plan include:
    • Cultural Centre
    • Public gathering place
    • Parks and trails
  • Recreation and Sports: Green spaces, walking paths, and places for sports and wellness.
  • Education and Learning: Schools and cultural learning centers for all ages.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Master Plan PDF

Why Naawi-Oodena Matters

This site sets a new standard for Indigenous urban development. It empowers local communities, honors history, and strengthens the connection between Indigenous culture and the land.

What’s unique is that Naawi-Oodena combines First Nations traditional values and cultures with modern city planning processes like zoning, roads, and green infrastructure. It shows real partnership between Indigenous traditions and urban planning. This model can influence future projects across Canada and beyond.

Sources: Treaty One on YouTube

The Finished Product

Naawi-Oodena has become a thriving, modern community that blends Indigenous culture with urban living. As Canada’s largest urban reserve, it now stands as a powerful symbol of reconciliation and self-determination for the Treaty One First Nations.

The residential area features a wide variety of homes, including detached houses, townhomes, stacked townhouses, and apartment buildings. Families of all sizes found suitable housing, with many units designed to be affordable and inclusive.

The partnership between Treaty One, Canada Lands Company, and the City of Winnipeg ensured that a strong mix of market-rate and affordable housing was available from the start. The first residential phase was a major success, adding 260 housing units just north of Taylor Avenue.

The design encouraged community engagement with walking paths, green spaces, and a layout that connects seamlessly with nearby neighborhoods like Tuxedo.
