The Completed Naawi-Oodena

Source: CNN - Canada's largest urban reserve opens in Winnipeg (2023)

Naawi-Oodena ("Centre of the Heart" in Anishinaabemowin) is now fully realized as a vibrant 156-acre urban reserve on the former Kapyong Barracks site in Winnipeg. This $1.2 billion completed development represents the largest single return of urban land to First Nations in Canadian history.

The development, led by Treaty One Nation, is now home to over 8,000 residents and features world-class commercial, cultural, and community spaces that honor Indigenous traditions while providing modern urban living.

Source: Treaty One Nation Annual Report (2025)

Residential Living

Our completed residential areas now house over 8,000 residents in a mix of:

  • 1,200 single-family homes
  • 900 townhouse units
  • 600 stacked townhomes
  • 500 apartment units (30% affordable)

All designed with Indigenous principles and sustainable features that have reduced energy use by 40% compared to conventional developments.

Source: Naawi-Oodena Residential Report (2025)

Thriving Commercial District

Our completed 1.5 million sq ft commercial space includes:

  • 200 Indigenous-owned businesses
